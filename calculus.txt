Calculus:
[Calculus] is the study of [Derivative]s and [Integral]s.

Rate of Change:
Calculating [Rate of Change] is like calculating speed, you take
the distance divided by time. <p>
In mathematics, a [Rate of Change] is the change in the y
value divided by the change in the x value. This is typically
depicted by the slope of the line between two points. <p>
In [Calculus], specifically, this is depicted as the slope
of the line tangent to the function at that point.

Limit:
A [Limit] can be calculated intuitively by definition. <p>
In particular, in the case of [Calculus], we take the [Limit] to 0.
When we do so, we treat the variable in question as an [Infinitesimal].

Infinitesimal:
An [Infinitesimal], per <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infinitesimal">
Wikipedia Definition</a>, is "a quantity that is closer to zero than any standard
real number, but is not zero." <p>
In terms of calculation, an [Infinitesimal] can be treated as zero, but can also
be divided (e.g. an [Infinitesimal] divided by itself is 1).

Instantaneous Rate of Change:
[Instantaneous Rate of Change] is what defines [Derivative]s. <p>
[Instantaneous Rate of Change] is essentially [Rate of Change],
but the speed [Limit]s to 0.

Derivative:
A [Derivative] is an [Instantaneous Rate of Change]. <p>
It can be calculated using [Derivative Rules]. <p>
